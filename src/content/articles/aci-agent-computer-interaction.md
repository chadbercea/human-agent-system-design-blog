## Hook

What happens when humans aren't in the loop? Pure agent-to-agent and agent-to-system workflows where the "H" in HCI disappears.

## Thesis

Agent-to-agent and agent-to-system interactions are not HCI with extra steps. They are a distinct discipline that needs its own frameworks, methods, and vocabulary.

## Key Points

- ACI as a child discipline of HCI.
- What the ACI layer looks like — YAML configs, MCP servers, tool schemas, protocol design.
- How ACI differs from HCI — specifications vs. psychology, protocols vs. mental models.
- Why this matters — pure autonomous agent coordination is already happening at scale.
- ACI still serves human purposes but operates without human presence.

## Payoff

Legitimize ACI as its own discipline and clarify when you're working at the ACI layer vs. the HCI layer.
