## Hook

Why user journey maps break when you try to map agent workflows. The moment you realize your UX toolkit doesn't have the right tool.

## Thesis

Designing agentic orchestrations requires service blueprints that show frontstage (human touchpoints), backstage (agent operations), support systems, handoffs, and intervention points.

## Key Points

- Why traditional UX artifacts fail — journey maps, empathy maps, wireframes.
- Service blueprint components adapted for orchestrations.
- Complete worked example: the design-to-code pipeline.
  - **Frontstage:** Chad creates designs, reviews deploys.
  - **Backstage:** Agents extract tokens, generate code, create PRs, auto-merge.
  - **Support systems:** Figma API, GitHub, MCP servers, CI/CD.
  - **Intervention points:** where Chad can pause or override.
- How to identify handoffs and failure modes.

## Payoff

A practical methodology designers can use immediately.
