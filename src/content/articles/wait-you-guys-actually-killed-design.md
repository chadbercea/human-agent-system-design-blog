Red line going DOWN: "Belief that design is dead because of Google Stitch"

At the very top of the blue line sits Alan Dye. Labeled "senior designer." The implication is clear: the more you climb, the more you understand design, the less you think design is dead.

Except Alan Dye ran Apple's UI design into the ground for a decade.

No UI design background. Came from Kate Spade - fashion branding, not interaction design. Spent ten years making superficial aesthetic choices while Apple's interface became increasingly unusable. When he left Apple for Meta in December 2025, the Apple design community celebrated. Not mourned. Celebrated. "The arsonist has left the crime scene" was an actual comment on the news.

So if the pinnacle of "understanding design as a function" is Alan Dye - a fraud with no taste, no craft, no knowledge who got promoted because he had the right credentials and said yes to the right people - then the chart accidentally proves something devastating:

**Design IS dead. And the best designers ARE idiots.**

Credential worship produces incompetence at the top. The people most confident design isn't dead are the ones actively killing it. Senior title means nothing. Years of experience means nothing. Being "Head of Design" at a prestigious company means nothing if you're just decorating decisions someone else made.

This is Position A. The system is broken. And the chart exposed it perfectly.

## Your Bonsai Is Dead (You Killed It)

Design in Western tech isn't dead because of AI. It's dying like an abused bonsai.

Picture a bonsai tree. Centuries-old growth potential compressed into artistic form through careful cultivation. The caretaker understands the tree - knows when to prune for health, when to let it grow, how to shape without destroying.

Now picture an idiot who bought a bonsai because it looked cool. Doesn't understand what they have. Just knows it's supposed to be small and aesthetic. So they start clipping. Shaping. Constraining.

Not based on the tree's health. Not based on growth patterns. Based on what they think it should look like. Based on org politics. Based on quarterly targets. Based on whatever bullshit metric PE firms use to justify strip-mining companies for parts.

Eventually, what's left barely resembles a tree. It's toothpicks. But they still call it a bonsai and sell it as a premium product.

**That's what happened to design.**

PE/VC touches everything and everything dies. Strip for parts. Maximize extraction. Leave a husk. Corporate structure promotes credential worship over competence - which feeds Position A and gives us Alan Dyes at every level.

Decision makers with ChatGPT PRDs calling themselves strategists. Executives who value speed over intent. Bootcamps mass-producing "designers" with no foundation. Tools companies convincing everyone that Figma skills equal design skills.

The evidence is everywhere:

- 150,000+ tech layoffs in 2024, design hit hardest
- Entry-level design hiring down 50%
- Only ONE Fortune 500 CDO hired in late 2023 (PayPal)
- Design leadership roles disappearing
- IDEO lost half its headcount, revenue dropped from $300M to $100M

This isn't AI displacement. This is the bonsai finally dying from years of abuse. The caretakers who never understood what they had kept clipping away everything essential until nothing functional remains.

And they're still pointing at the toothpicks calling it a bonsai.

This is Position B. The discipline is dying from abuse by incompetent caretakers.

## What Design Actually Is (The Thing That Survives)

Here's what design is. Not what it looks like. Not what tools you use. Not what your LinkedIn bio says.

**Design is the intent behind decisions for specific outcomes.**

That's it. That's the atomic definition. Functionally, philosophically, and rationally constant.

Not the tools. Figma, React, pen and paper, AI, cave paintings - doesn't matter. Tools change every decade. The definition doesn't.

Not the artifacts. Mockups, prototypes, code, presentations, reports - these are byproducts of thinking, not the thinking itself.

Not the org structure. Upstream, downstream, embedded, waterfall, agile, dual-track, triple-diamond, whatever process theater you're performing - irrelevant to the definition.

Not the cultural moment. Western tech, AI era, post-AI era, whatever comes next - the core remains constant.

**Intent -> Decisions -> Outcomes.**

Rick Rubin never played an instrument. Yet every musician seeks his direction. Why? Because he operates with intent toward outcomes. He understands what the artist is trying to create and makes decisions that enable that outcome. Strategy over execution. Direction over production.

Are you Rick Rubin? Or are you the session musician who only shows up when someone tells you what to play?

Here's the test:

**Are you making intentional decisions toward specific outcomes?**

Or are you producing artifacts someone else decided you should make?

If you're not sure which one you are, you're producing artifacts.

This is Position C. This is what design actually is. Always has been. Always will be. The bonsai can die. The frauds can keep getting promoted. The industry can collapse into toothpick merchants selling credentials. But this definition survives because it's true.
